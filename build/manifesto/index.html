001 · Who

Goes by Klee.

Autodidact. Curiosity-driven. Obsessive by nature.

002 · Why

Africa doesn't need
more spectators.
It needs builders.

The tools exist. The internet exists. The knowledge exists. What's missing is the courage to use them without waiting for a certificate, a salary, or someone else's validation.

Everything I build is free until it can't be. Because the version of me that needed this couldn't afford to pay for it.

003 · Background Thoughts

Things I keep
coming back to.

01
The classroom teaches you to pass. The market teaches you to build. Nobody told us these were different subjects.
02
Experience isn't given. It's manufactured — deliberately, obsessively, before anyone thinks you're ready.
03
Africa doesn't catch up. It leaps. The infrastructure gap is an advantage — we skip the broken steps entirely.
04
AI is not the threat. Waiting to understand AI before using it — that's the threat.
05
Ship it or kill it. There is no third option. "In progress forever" is just failure with better branding.
06
Equanimity is not peace. It's the ability to build through chaos without needing the chaos to stop first.
07
Be first and foremost a person of value. The network, the clients, the wins — they follow eventually.
08
Africa is overflowing with such wealth it can barely contain itself and periodically showers people with money to prune its excess.
09
"The hottest new programming language is English." — Andrej Karpathy
10
What stands in the way becomes the way.
11
Not wanting something is as good as having it.
